2. Dwell Time

What it is: How long fans spend engaging with sponsor content

Why it matters: A 2-second logo view â‰  a 30-second branded experience

How to measure: Average time spent on sponsor-related content/experiences

Real Example: Minor League Baseball

Average fan spent 42 seconds watching sponsor-branded player highlights via NFC tap

Sponsor value: 42 seconds of focused attention vs. seconds-long banner exposure

3. Geographic Distribution

What it is: Where your engaged fans are located

Why it matters: Sponsors want to know if they're reaching their target markets

How to measure: Location data from digital interactions (with consent)

Real Example: Regional College

Discovered 37% of engaged fans were from out-of-state alumni, not local market

Sponsor value: Helped regional sponsor justify expansion into new markets

4. Repeat Engagement

What it is: How many times fans come back to interact with sponsor content

Why it matters: Shows lasting impact, not one-time exposure

How to measure: Average number of interactions per fan over time

Real Example: Private University

Alumni engaged with class year content an average of 6.3 times over 18 months

Sponsor value: 6.3 brand touchpoints vs. single logo placement

5. Conversion Events

What it is: Measurable actions fans take after engaging with sponsor

Why it matters: Directly ties sponsorship to business results

How to measure: Track clicks, sign-ups, purchases, visits, etc.

Real Example: Community College

89 fans clicked through to sponsor's website; 23 signed up for offer

Sponsor value: Direct attribution from sponsorship to customer acquisition

How to Present ROI to Sponsors

Create Sponsor Reports

Provide sponsors with regular reports showing their metrics. Include:

  • Total engagement count
  • Engagement rate vs. benchmark
  • Geographic breakdown
  • Trend over time
  • Conversion events (if trackable)

The Monthly Report Template

ðŸ“Š Monthly Sponsor Report Structure

  1. Executive Summary - Top 3 wins this month
  2. Engagement Metrics - Numbers + visuals
  3. Fan Stories - Qualitative feedback
  4. Competitive Benchmark - How you compare
  5. Next Month - What's coming

Renewal Conversations

When it's time to renew, lead with:

  1. Total value delivered: "2,847 engaged fans over 12 months"
  2. Cost per engagement: "$17.60 per engaged fan vs. industry average of $45"
  3. Outcomes achieved: "23 direct conversions, 89 website visits"
  4. What's new: "Next year we're adding X capability"
