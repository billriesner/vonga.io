What You're Sending Today

Impressions estimates. Attendance figures. Logo placement photos. Sponsors nod politely but the data doesn't answer the question they're actually asking.

Verified Fan Engagement Data

Not estimates. Not impressions. Verified interactions tied to real fan behavior — down to the individual tap level.

Total Verified Engagements

What it is: The exact number of fans who interacted with a sponsor's activation — tap events logged, timestamped, and device-verified.

Why it matters: Sponsors can't dispute a verified engagement count the way they can dispute an impressions estimate. This is real.

Fan Demographics

What it is: Geographic breakdown, engagement time patterns, device type, and behavioral segments of the fans who engaged with the activation.

Why it matters: Sponsors want to know they're reaching the right audience. This shows them exactly who engaged — not who was at the game.

Engagement Timeline

What it is: When engagement happened — day by day, event by event. See peaks during specific games, announcements, or content drops.

Why it matters: Shows sponsors their activation has a lifespan beyond game day. Physical items keep getting tapped for weeks and months.

Content Interaction Rates

What it is: When fans tap to a sponsor's branded content — did they click through? How long did they engage? What did they do next?

Why it matters: Moves the conversation from "your logo was seen" to "here's what fans did with your activation."

Geographic Reach

What it is: Where fans were when they engaged with the sponsor's content — in-venue, at home, in other cities, across regions.

Why it matters: Sponsors targeting specific geographic markets see exactly what physical reach their activation generated.

Repeat Engagement Rate

What it is: What percentage of fans engaged with the same sponsor activation more than once? Which fans are the most loyal to the sponsor's content?

Why it matters: Repeat engagement is the strongest signal of genuine brand connection — and you can prove it now.
