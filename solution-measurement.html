The Measurement Gap

Flying Blind

Traditional merch provides no feedback loop. You sell shirts, but have no idea if anyone actually cares.

Data That Actually Matters

Not vanity metrics. Real engagement data that tells you what's working, what's not, and what to do next.

Engagement Rates

What We Track: How many people tap their apparel. How often they come back. How long they engage with content.

Why It Matters: Traditional merch has zero ongoing engagement. Industry benchmarks show NFC engagement of 15-40%, with multiple taps per garment over time.

What You Learn: Which content resonates. Which experiences drive repeat interaction. What fans actually care about.

Geographic Distribution

What We Track: Where fans are when they tap. Concentration areas. Travel patterns. Regional preferences.

Why It Matters: One program discovered 40% of engagement came from out-of-state alumni, reshaping their entire merchandise strategy.

What You Learn: Where your real fanbase lives. Where to focus marketing. Where to schedule events.

Content Performance

What We Track: Which videos get watched. Which messages get read. Which experiences get shared.

Why It Matters: Know what content works before investing more resources. Double down on winners, kill losers.

What You Learn: What stories resonate. Which voices fans trust. How to improve content strategy.

Audience Segmentation

What We Track: Student vs. alumni engagement. Donor behavior. Recruit interaction patterns.

Why It Matters: Different audiences need different content. Mass marketing wastes resources on irrelevant messages.

What You Learn: How to personalize experiences. Which segments need attention. Where to allocate resources.

Fan Journey Mapping

What We Track: First tap to repeat engagement. Content progression. Behavior over time.

Why It Matters: Understand how casual fans become loyal supporters. Map the journey, optimize the path.

What You Learn: What drives loyalty. Where fans drop off. How to improve retention.

Revenue Attribution

What We Track: Premium pricing acceptance. Upsell conversion. Ongoing revenue per fan.

Why It Matters: Prove technology pays for itself. Show clear ROI to justify continued investment.

What You Learn: What fans will pay for. How to optimize pricing. Where revenue opportunity exists.
