# Homepage Blueprint — 10-Section Narrative Sequence

## Overview
The homepage follows a precise narrative sequence designed to move visitors from curiosity → recognition → understanding → trust → conversion.

**Total target time on page:** 90-180 seconds  
**Conversion goal:** Qualified lead form submission

---

## Section 1: Hero
**Purpose:** Hook + positioning + primary CTA  
**Time:** 0-5 seconds  
**Emotional goal:** Curiosity + pattern interrupt

**Required elements:**
- Headline: Hook that positions Vonga as category-defining
- Subhead: One-sentence category translation
- Visual: Premium kit in context (fans, game, community)
- Dual CTAs: "Let's Connect" (primary) + "See How It Works" (scroll to video)
- Background: Video loop or hero image

**Copy framework:**
- Headline: Category-defining statement (not just "better apparel")
- Subhead: Plain-English translation of what Vonga is
- Keep it under 20 words total

**Success metric:** Visitor doesn't bounce, scrolls to section 2

---

## Section 2: The Problem (Continuity Problem)
**Purpose:** Name the pain that visitors feel but haven't articulated  
**Time:** 5-15 seconds  
**Emotional goal:** Recognition + empathy ("Finally, someone gets it")

**Required elements:**
- Headline: "The Continuity Problem" or similar framing
- Problem statement: "Merch ends at checkout"
- Contrast: Transaction vs Relationship
- Visual: Split imagery showing transaction end vs ongoing connection

**Copy framework:**
- Core belief: "Most teams don't have a fan problem. They have a continuity problem."
- Enemy: Transaction-only merch partnerships / static engagement
- Make the invisible visible: name what they're feeling

**Success metric:** Visitor nods in recognition, continues scrolling

---

## Section 3: Clothes vs Connection (Contrast Table)
**Purpose:** Clear category shift through side-by-side comparison  
**Time:** 15-30 seconds  
**Emotional goal:** "This is different"

**Required elements:**
- Two-column comparison table
- Left column: "Everyone Else Sells Clothes" (transactional)
- Right column: "Vonga Sells Connection" (relational)
- 4-6 comparison points focused on economics and outcomes

**Comparison points (examples):**
| Everyone Else | Vonga |
|--------------|--------|
| One-time sale | Ongoing channel |
| Volume metrics | Revenue per fan |
| Ends at checkout | Starts at checkout |
| Static apparel | Living connection |
| Passive merch | Active engagement infrastructure |

**Success metric:** Visitor understands category difference

---

## Section 4: What Vonga Is (Category Definition)
**Purpose:** Translate the category into plain English  
**Time:** 30-45 seconds  
**Emotional goal:** Understanding + credibility

**Required elements:**
- Category name: "Living Engagement Infrastructure"
- Plain-English translation: "A complete fan engagement channel, delivered through premium team apparel."
- One-sentence explanation: "Vonga turns what people wear into a living connection, unlocking access, rewards, and experiences every time they show up."
- Proof mechanism: "Because apparel is the one thing people actually bring with them."

**Copy framework:**
- Start with category name
- Immediately translate to operator language
- Explain the "why now" (apparel as always-present channel)
- Brief mention of NFC as mechanism only if needed

**Success metric:** Visitor can explain Vonga to a colleague

---

## Section 5: Video Demo
**Purpose:** Show don't tell, proof of concept  
**Time:** 45-135 seconds (if watched)  
**Emotional goal:** "I can see this working"

**Required elements:**
- Video player (60-90 second demo)
- Placeholder: Gray container with play icon and text "Demo video coming soon"
- Optional: Thumbnail image showing fan tapping kit
- Caption: What the video demonstrates

**Video content (when ready):**
- Fan wearing kit at event
- Tap to unlock experience
- Examples of experience types
- Team dashboard view
- Revenue/engagement metrics

**Success metric:** Video play rate >40%, completion rate >60%

---

## Section 6: Experience Types
**Purpose:** Showcase platform versatility and use cases  
**Time:** 135-165 seconds  
**Emotional goal:** "This solves multiple problems"

**Required elements:**
Five experience type cards (exact list):

1. **Geofenced Experiences**
   - Turn any place into a team activation
   - Examples: Scavenger hunts at games/races, watch parties worldwide
   
2. **Time-Bound Experiences**
   - Engage anytime
   - Examples: During games/races, team announcements, drops, custom experiences

3. **Exclusive Content**
   - A channel beyond socials
   - Examples: BTS, special messages from players, community for kit holders

4. **Sales Channel for Digital Products**
   - Physical item as key to access other items
   - Examples: Digital products, measurable revenue per fan

5. **Sponsor Platform**
   - Increase sponsor value
   - Examples: Deliver offers/experiences to verified kit holders

**Layout:** Grid of 5 cards, icon + title + description per card

**Success metric:** Visitor sees multiple relevant use cases

---

## Section 7: Partnership (White-Glove Service)
**Purpose:** Address "will this work for us?" concern  
**Time:** 165-180 seconds  
**Emotional goal:** "We'll be supported"

**Required elements:**
- Headline: Partnership approach
- Key points:
  - Hands-on partnership (not transactional)
  - True customization and design collaboration
  - Co-creation process
  - Dedicated support
- Visual: Team collaboration or design process image

**Copy framework:**
- Emphasize partnership over platform
- Address the "overlooked by big brands" positioning
- Highlight customization capabilities
- Make it feel bespoke, not cookie-cutter

**Success metric:** Visitor feels this is for teams like theirs

---

## Section 8: Trust / Risk Removal
**Purpose:** Remove objections and build credibility  
**Time:** 180-195 seconds  
**Emotional goal:** "This is low-risk and credible"

**Required trust statements (all must appear):**
1. ✓ No personal data collected
2. ✓ No app required
3. ✓ No QR codes / no QR fallback
4. ✓ Permanent, wash-tested tags (no replacements)
5. ✓ Cannot be cloned
6. ✓ Team-controlled experiences
7. ✓ Experience can be hosted on team domain or Vonga

**Layout:** Icon + statement grid, clear checkmarks, simple language

**Success metric:** Visitor's objections are addressed

---

## Section 9: Lead Form
**Purpose:** Qualify and convert visitors into leads  
**Time:** 195-240 seconds  
**Emotional goal:** "I'd regret not exploring this"

**Required elements:**
- Headline: Value-focused ("Let's explore if Vonga is right for your team")
- Form fields (keep low friction):
  - Name (required)
  - Email (required)
  - Team/Organization (required)
  - Role (optional dropdown)
  - Message/Interest (optional textarea)
- Submit button: **"Submit"** (NOT "Let's Connect")
- Privacy note: Brief, reassuring

**Form validation:**
- Client-side with clear error messages
- Email format validation
- Required field indicators

**Success metric:** Form submission (primary conversion goal)

---

## Section 10: Footer CTA
**Purpose:** Last conversion opportunity  
**Time:** 240+ seconds  
**Emotional goal:** Final nudge for those still exploring

**Required elements:**
- Gradient background (aqua → coral)
- Headline: Concise, action-oriented
- CTA button: "Let's Connect"
- Footer navigation (minimal)

**Copy framework:**
- One sentence that summarizes asymmetric upside
- Example: "Turn what your fans wear into your most valuable channel."

**Success metric:** Catch visitors who scrolled past form

---

## Overall Flow Metrics

**Scroll depth targets:**
- 25% (past hero + problem) → 80% of visitors
- 50% (past contrast + definition) → 60% of visitors
- 75% (past experiences) → 40% of visitors
- 100% (to form) → 25% of visitors

**Conversion funnel:**
- Visit → 100%
- Scroll to form → 25%
- Form start → 15%
- Form submit → 5-8%

---

## Design Consistency Rules

**Spacing between sections:** 80-128px  
**Max content width:** 1280px  
**Section padding:** 64-96px vertical  
**Typography hierarchy:**
- Section headline: 36-48px
- Section subhead: 18-24px
- Body: 16-18px

**Color usage:**
- Navy for primary headlines
- Aqua for accents and CTAs
- Coral for emphasis and secondary CTAs
- White backgrounds with occasional gray-100 alternation
