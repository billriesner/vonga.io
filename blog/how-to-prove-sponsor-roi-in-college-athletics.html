How to Prove Sponsor ROI in College Athletics: The 5 Metrics That Actually Matter

Bill Riesner
February 1, 2026 · 9 min read

The Sponsor ROI Crisis in College Athletics

If you're an athletic director or marketing director at a college or university, you've probably had this conversation:

Sponsor: "We're paying $50,000 for our logo on your stadium. What's the ROI?"

You: "We had 5,000 people at the game last Saturday."

Sponsor: "And how many of them saw our logo? For how long? Did anyone engage with our brand?"

You: "..."

This conversation is happening at athletic departments across the country. Sponsors are getting smarter, budgets are tighter, and "we had X impressions" doesn't cut it anymore.1

The question is: What should you be measuring instead?

Why Traditional Metrics Are Failing

For years, athletic programs reported sponsor value using metrics like:

  • Attendance numbers ("5,000 fans in the stadium")
  • Impressions ("10,000 people saw your logo")
  • Social media reach ("Our post got 2,000 views")
  • Email subscribers ("We sent your message to 8,000 people")

The problem? None of these prove engagement.

  • Attendance ≠ saw the sponsor logo
  • Impressions ≠ paid attention
  • Social media reach ≠ actual views
  • Email sent ≠ email read

Sponsors know this. They've been burned by "impressions" that meant nothing, "reach" that never reached anyone, and "awareness" campaigns that created zero business value.

Now they're asking harder questions. And if you can't answer them, they're not renewing.

The 5 Metrics Sponsors Actually Care About

Based on our work with athletic programs and conversations with dozens of sponsors, here are the five metrics that actually matter:

1. Engagement Rate (Not Impressions)

What It Is: The percentage of your audience that actively interacted with sponsor content.

Why It Matters: Shows real attention, not passive exposure. A 1,000-person audience with 60% engagement is more valuable than a 10,000-person audience with 1% engagement.

How to Measure:

Engagement Rate = (Active Interactions ÷ Total Audience) × 100

Example:
You distribute 500 NFC-enabled shirts at homecoming. 312 fans tap their phones to access sponsor content.
Engagement Rate: 62.4%

Benchmark: NFC-enabled merchandise typically achieves 40-60% engagement rates2, compared to QR codes (1-5% for passive campaigns, 10-15% with active promotion) and organic social media posts (<2% engagement)3. This 8-30× performance advantage makes NFC the most effective technology for proving sponsor ROI in physical environments.

2. Dwell Time (Not Views)

What It Is: How long fans spend engaging with sponsor content.

Why It Matters: A 2-second logo view ≠ a 30-second branded experience. Sponsors want focused attention, not scroll-past exposure. Research shows that attention time directly correlates with brand recall and purchase intent4.

How to Measure: Average time spent on sponsor-related content or experiences (trackable with digital platforms).

Example:
Fans tap NFC-enabled apparel to watch a sponsor-branded highlight reel. Average watch time: 42 seconds.
Dwell Time: 42 seconds of focused attention per engaged fan.

Benchmark: 30-60 seconds average dwell time indicates strong engagement.

3. Geographic Distribution (Not Total Reach)

What It Is: Where your engaged fans are located.

Why It Matters: Sponsors want to know if they're reaching their target markets. A national reach doesn't help a local sponsor. A local reach doesn't help a national sponsor.

How to Measure: Location data from digital interactions (with appropriate consent and privacy compliance per GDPR/CCPA regulations).

Example:
A regional sponsor targets the tri-state area. Your engagement data shows 67% of engaged fans are from their target region.
Geographic Match: 67% (proves sponsor is reaching the right market)

Benchmark: Aim for >50% overlap with sponsor's target market.

4. Repeat Engagement (Not One-Time Exposure)

What It Is: How many times fans come back to interact with sponsor content over time.

Why It Matters: One-time exposure creates minimal impact. Marketing research consistently shows that 7+ brand touchpoints are needed to drive action5. Repeated touchpoints build brand recall and drive conversion.

How to Measure: Average number of interactions per fan over a defined period (season, year, etc.).

Example:
Fans engage with NFC-enabled apparel an average of 4.2 times over a season (initial tap + 3.2 return engagements).
Repeat Engagement: 4.2 touchpoints per fan

Benchmark: 3+ repeat engagements indicates lasting value.

5. Conversion Events (Not "Awareness")

What It Is: Measurable actions fans take after engaging with sponsor content.

Why It Matters: Directly ties sponsorship to business results. Sponsors don't just want awareness—they want clicks, sign-ups, purchases, visits.

How to Measure: Track specific actions:

  • Website visits (via trackable links)
  • Sign-ups (email list, loyalty program, etc.)
  • Purchases (promo code usage)
  • Store visits (location-based tracking)
  • App downloads

Example:
89 fans clicked through to sponsor's website from NFC-enabled apparel. 23 signed up for sponsor's offer.
Conversion Events: 89 clicks, 23 sign-ups (25.8% conversion rate)

Benchmark: Even a small percentage of conversions proves direct business impact. Typical digital conversion rates range from 2-5%6, so 25% indicates highly qualified traffic.

How to Actually Measure These Metrics

Here's the challenge: traditional sponsor activations can't measure any of this.

  • Stadium signage? Unmeasurable.
  • Logo on a shirt? No data.
  • Social media post? Vanity metrics at best.
  • Email blast? Open rates don't prove engagement.

To measure the 5 metrics above, you need trackable digital touchpoints. Here's how programs are doing it:

Option 1: NFC-Enabled Apparel

Fans tap their phone to the garment (no app required—NFC is built into all modern smartphones7) and access sponsor content. Every tap is tracked:

  • Who engaged (demographics, if consented)
  • When they engaged (timestamp)
  • Where they engaged (location data)
  • How long they engaged (dwell time)
  • What they viewed (content performance)
  • Repeat engagement (how many times they came back)

Engagement Rate: 40-60% (based on aggregate campaign data)
Implementation: 45 days from contract to launch
Cost per Unit: $1.50-$2.00 per NFC chip + platform fee

Revenue Opportunity

NFC-enabled apparel isn't just a sponsor engagement tool—it's a profit center for your program. Teams can retail NFC-enabled merchandise at premium pricing while maintaining healthy margins.

Example Economics:

  • Production cost: $15 per NFC-enabled t-shirt (garment + chip + printing)
  • Retail price: $40
  • Profit per unit: $25

If you sell 500 units at a game or event, that's $12,500 in direct revenue to your program—on top of the sponsor engagement value. The apparel pays for itself while delivering measurable sponsor ROI.

Option 2: QR Codes (Lower Performance Alternative)

Fans scan QR code to access digital content. Trackable, but significantly lower engagement rates.

Engagement Rate: 1-5% (typical for passive QR code campaigns; higher rates of 10-15% possible with active promotion and incentives8)
Implementation: Immediate
Cost: Free (but requires content platform)

Why the Engagement Gap Matters:

The difference between NFC and QR engagement rates dramatically impacts sponsor value. Consider this scenario:

1,000 fans receive branded merchandise with sponsor content:

Technology Engagement Rate Fans Engaged Sponsor Value
NFC-enabled apparel 40-60% 400-600 fans High measurable ROI
QR code printing 1-5% 10-50 fans Minimal measurable impact

The Math: With NFC, a sponsor reaches 400-600 actively engaged fans. With QR codes, they reach 10-50. That's an 8-12× difference in measurable engagement from the same distribution.

For sponsors evaluating ROI, that gap is the difference between renewing at a higher rate and walking away.

Option 3: Custom Mobile App

Fans download your app, access sponsor content through the app.

Engagement Rate: <5% (app download is a significant barrier; 77% of users never download apps beyond their initial setup9)
Implementation: 3-6 months
Cost: $50k+ to build + ongoing maintenance

Option 4: Digital Kiosks/Screens

Fans interact with sponsor content at physical locations (stadium, arena, etc.).

Engagement Rate: Varies widely (10-30%)
Implementation: 2-3 months
Cost: $10k-$50k per kiosk

How to Get Started

Step 1: Audit Your Current Sponsor Reporting

Ask yourself:

  • What metrics do we report to sponsors now?
  • Can we prove engagement or just exposure?
  • Would our current data justify a renewal if we were the sponsor?

Step 2: Choose Your Measurement Method

Based on your budget, timeline, and program size:

  • High engagement, fast launch: NFC-enabled apparel
  • Low cost, DIY: QR codes
  • Long-term investment: Custom app
  • Event-specific: Digital kiosks

Step 3: Implement Tracking

Set up the technology (NFC platform, QR system, app, etc.) and ensure you're capturing:

  • Engagement rate
  • Dwell time
  • Geographic distribution
  • Repeat engagement
  • Conversion events

Step 4: Create Sponsor Reports

Provide sponsors with regular reports showing THEIR specific metrics. Include:

  • How many fans engaged with their content
  • How long fans spent with their brand
  • Where engaged fans are located
  • What content performed best
  • Direct conversions (if trackable)

Step 5: Use Data to Justify Renewal Rates

When renewal time comes, lead with data:

❌ "We had 5,000 fans at the game."

✅ "312 fans actively engaged with your brand for an average of 42 seconds. 78% were in your target market. 23 converted to sign-ups."

Which conversation do you want to have?

Common Objections (And How to Handle Them)

"This sounds expensive."

Response: Compared to what? Losing a $50k sponsor because you can't prove ROI? The real cost is what you lose by NOT measuring.

Example: A $15k investment in measurable engagement can justify a $15k sponsor increase. Payback period: 1 year.

"Our fans won't use it."

Response: NFC engagement rates average 40-60%, compared to <2% for social media and <5% for app downloads. Fans engage when it's easy and valuable.

Example: Athletic programs consistently report 3-5× higher engagement with NFC vs. traditional QR codes.

"We don't have time for this."

Response: Implementation timelines vary:

  • NFC platform: 45 days (turnkey)
  • QR codes: Immediate (DIY)
  • App: 3-6 months (complex)

Pick the option that fits your timeline. Doing nothing guarantees sponsor churn.

Conclusion

The days of reporting "impressions" and "reach" are over. Sponsors want proof of engagement, and athletic programs that can deliver it are winning renewals at higher rates.

  1. Engagement Rate
  2. Dwell Time
  3. Geographic Distribution
  4. Repeat Engagement
