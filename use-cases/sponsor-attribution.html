Sponsorship's Measurement Problem

You can't prove what you can't measure. And most sponsor activations can't be measured.

The Gap

Sponsors invest millions in jersey patches, stadium signage, branded giveaways, and activation zones. But when renewal time comes, what data do you have?

  • Media impressions (estimated, not verified)
  • Brand recall surveys (expensive, unreliable)
  • Social media mentions (incomplete)

None of this proves the sponsor actually reached fans or drove action.

The Cost

When you can't prove activation value, sponsors churn. Or worse — they downgrade.

The math: Losing a $500K sponsor because you can't prove their activation worked costs more than any measurement platform.

Conversely, proving ROI lets you justify premium pricing and expand partnerships.

NFC Makes Sponsorship Measurable

Every NFC-enabled sponsor touchpoint becomes a tracked interaction.

Branded Merch

"Presented by [Sponsor]" jerseys and apparel with embedded NFC. Every fan tap is attributed to the sponsor.

Data captured: Unique fans reached, tap frequency, geographic spread, engagement over time.

In-Venue Activations

Sponsor lounges, activation zones, and branded touchpoints with NFC. Fans tap to access exclusive content — sponsors get captured leads.

Data captured: Foot traffic conversion, lead capture rate, content engagement.

Sponsor Giveaways

Branded giveaway items with NFC create ongoing engagement. The rally towel keeps generating data weeks after game day.

Data captured: Post-event engagement, repeat taps, geographic distribution of sponsor reach.

The Data Sponsors Actually Want

Move beyond impressions to metrics that prove real engagement.

What They Get Today
