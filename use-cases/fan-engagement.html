The Fan Identity Gap

Teams have millions of fans but know almost none of them by name. That's the identity gap — and it's costing you revenue.

The Problem

80%+ of merch buyers are completely anonymous to teams. They wear your jersey, attend your games, and engage with your brand daily — but you can't identify them, segment them, or market to them directly.

Traditional digital tools only capture fans who download apps (3-5% of your fanbase) or fill out forms (even fewer). The physical world — where most fan engagement actually happens — generates zero data.

The Opportunity

NFC technology bridges the gap between physical fandom and digital identity. Every jersey, hat, scarf, and giveaway item becomes a data capture opportunity.

When fans tap NFC-enabled merch, they unlock exclusive content and you capture their identity. It's a value exchange that works: fans get something they want, you get the first-party data you need.

How Vonga Works

Three steps from physical merch to captured fan identity. No app downloads. No QR friction. Just tap and connect.

Smart Merch

NFC chips embedded in jerseys, hats, scarves, and premium apparel. Fans tap at home, at tailgates, or in their seats — whenever they want to engage with exclusive content.

Use cases: Season ticket holder jerseys, retail merch upgrades, VIP member gear, player-specific apparel.

Smart Venue

NFC touchpoints throughout your venue: concourse kiosks, seat-back tags, gate entry points, VIP lounges, concession stands, sponsor activation zones.

Use cases: Game day giveaways, loyalty check-ins, in-venue sweepstakes, sponsor activations.

What You Learn About Every Fan

Every tap enriches the fan profile. Progressive profiling builds over time as fans engage.

Identity Data

  • Name, email, phone (permission-based)
  • Social profiles (optional connection)
  • Device type and preferences

Behavioral Data

  • What they tap and when
  • Location of each interaction
  • Content consumption patterns
  • Repeat engagement frequency

Segmentation Intelligence

  • Engagement score rankings
  • Geographic heat maps
  • Item affinity analysis
  • Campaign response tracking
