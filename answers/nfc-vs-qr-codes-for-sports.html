NFC vs. QR Codes for Sports Marketing

The main difference is user friction. NFC (Near Field Communication) uses a simple tap from a smartphone to launch a digital experience, while QR codes require the user to open their camera, frame the code, and then click a link. This makes NFC significantly faster and more seamless.

This difference in user experience leads to much higher engagement rates for NFC—often cited as being 10-12 times more effective than QR codes. Furthermore, NFC tags can be embedded discreetly within products (like a jersey patch) and can be encrypted, making them more secure and aesthetically pleasing than a printed QR code.
