What is First-Party Data in Sports?

First-party data in sports is any information that a team collects directly from its fans with their explicit consent. This includes email addresses from a newsletter sign-up, purchase history from the team store, or engagement data from an NFC tap on a piece of merchandise. The key is that the team owns and controls this data.

This is different from third-party data, which is collected by another entity (like a social media platform or data broker). As privacy regulations tighten and cookies are phased out, collecting and owning first-party data has become critical for teams to understand their audience, personalize communication, and prove value to sponsors.
